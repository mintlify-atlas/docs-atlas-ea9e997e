Customer churn — the rate at which subscribers cancel or stop using a service — is one of the most closely watched metrics in the telecommunications industry. For TelecomX LATAM, an analysis of 7,267 customer records revealed a churn rate of **26.5%**, meaning roughly 1 in 4 customers left the company during the observed period.

## What Is Churn?

Churn (also called customer attrition or evasión de clientes) occurs when a customer ends their relationship with a service provider. In the telecom context this can mean:

- Cancelling a phone or internet subscription
- Switching to a competing provider
- Failing to renew a contract at expiration

The `Churn` field in the TelecomX dataset captures this as a binary label — `"Yes"` for customers who left and `"No"` for those who stayed — making it the **target variable** for any predictive model built on top of this data.

## Why Churn Matters

Retaining an existing customer is significantly cheaper than acquiring a new one. Industry estimates commonly place customer acquisition cost (CAC) at five to seven times the cost of retention. For a company with a 26.5% annual churn rate, the compounding effect on revenue is substantial:

## Key Risk Factors Identified

Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) on the TelecomX dataset surfaced three primary churn drivers. Each maps directly to a variable in the data dictionary.

### 1. Month-to-Month Contracts

The `Contract` field distinguishes between `"Month-to-month"`, `"One year"`, and `"Two year"` agreements. Customers on month-to-month contracts showed the highest churn concentration in the dataset — this group faces zero switching cost at any billing cycle, making them the most at-risk segment.

### 2. Early Tenure (First 6 Months)

The `tenure` field records how many months a customer has been with TelecomX. Analysis revealed that the **first 6 months** of a customer relationship represent the highest-risk window. Customers who survive beyond this initial period show markedly lower churn probability, suggesting that early engagement and onboarding quality are critical levers.

### 3. Absence of Tech Support Subscription

The `TechSupport` field indicates whether a customer has subscribed to the additional tech support service (reduced wait times). Customers **without** this add-on were strongly correlated with higher churn rates. This may reflect dissatisfaction with base-level support responsiveness, or it may indicate lower overall engagement with TelecomX's service ecosystem.

<Warning>
  These three factors are correlated risk indicators, not proven causal drivers. A customer on a month-to-month contract with low tenure and no tech support subscription should be treated as high-priority for retention outreach, but further modeling is needed to quantify individual risk scores.
</Warning>

## How EDA Identifies Churn Drivers

Exploratory Data Analysis is the process of summarizing and visualizing a dataset before formal modeling. In this project, EDA was performed using **Pandas**, **Matplotlib**, and **Seaborn** on the flattened TelecomX dataset. The core techniques applied were:

- **Distribution analysis** — comparing the distribution of each variable between churned and retained customers (e.g., tenure histograms split by `Churn`)
- **Categorical counts** — bar charts of `Contract`, `InternetService`, `PaymentMethod`, and add-on subscriptions grouped by churn status
- **Correlation heatmaps** — identifying which numeric features (`tenure`, `Charges.Monthly`, `Charges.Total`) move together with the churn label
- **Feature engineering** — derived metrics such as daily spend rate and total number of contracted services to surface patterns not visible in raw fields

EDA does not produce a predictive model, but it narrows the hypothesis space considerably — enabling data scientists to focus modeling effort on variables with demonstrated discriminative power.

## Business Impact of Reducing Churn

Even a modest reduction in churn rate translates to meaningful revenue recovery. Consider a simplified scenario based on the TelecomX dataset proportions:

```
Baseline churned customers:  ~1,926
Average monthly charge:       ~$65 (approximate dataset median)

If churn rate drops from 26.5% → 23.5% (3 pp reduction):
  Customers retained:  ~218 additional
  Monthly revenue recovered:  ~$14,170
  Annual revenue recovered:   ~$170,000
```

<Tip>
  The highest-leverage retention interventions target the overlap of all three risk factors: customers who are on month-to-month contracts, have less than 6 months of tenure, and have not subscribed to tech support. This cohort can be identified directly from the TelecomX dataset fields without requiring a predictive model.
</Tip>
