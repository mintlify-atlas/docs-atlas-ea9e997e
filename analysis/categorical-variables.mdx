## Overview

Analyzing churn across categorical variables reveals which customer segments are most at risk. The notebook examines four primary categorical dimensions: gender, senior citizen status, contract type, and tech support subscription.

## Visualization Code

The core visualization uses grouped countplots to show churn vs. no-churn counts for each category:

```python
import matplotlib.pyplot as plt
import seaborn as sns

# 1. Selección de variables categóricas clave para el análisis
vars_interes = ['Genero', 'Adulto_Mayor', 'Tipo_Contrato', 'Soporte_Tecnico']

# 2. Configuración de la cuadrícula de subgráficos (2x2)
fig, axes = plt.subplots(nrows=2, ncols=2, figsize=(16, 12))
axes = axes.flatten()

for i, var in enumerate(vars_interes):
    # Gráfico de barras agrupado por la variable de Abandono (Churn)
    sns.countplot(data=df_telecom_es, x=var, hue='Abandono',
                  palette='viridis', ax=axes[i])

    axes[i].set_title(f'Distribución de Abandono por {var}', fontsize=14, fontweight='bold')
    axes[i].set_xlabel(var)
    axes[i].set_ylabel('Número de Clientes')
    axes[i].legend(title='Abandono', labels=['No', 'Sí'])

plt.tight_layout()
plt.show()

# 3. Análisis de proporciones numéricas para el Tipo de Contrato (el más crítico)
print("### PORCENTAJE DE ABANDONO POR TIPO DE CONTRATO ###")
prop_contrato = df_telecom_es.groupby('Tipo_Contrato')['Abandono'].mean() * 100
print(prop_contrato.sort_values(ascending=False))
```

## Contract Type Analysis

Contract type is the single most predictive categorical variable for churn.

**Output:**
```
### PORCENTAJE DE ABANDONO POR TIPO DE CONTRATO ###
Tipo_Contrato
Month-to-month    42.71%
One year          11.27%
Two year           2.83%
```

| Contract Type | Churn Rate | Share of Customers |
|---------------|------------|--------------------|
| Month-to-month | **42.71%** | 55.0% |
| One year | 11.27% | 20.9% |
| Two year | 2.83% | 24.1% |

<Warning>
More than half of all customers are on month-to-month contracts — and those customers churn at a rate 15× higher than two-year contract holders. This is the highest-priority retention lever in the entire dataset.
</Warning>

The pattern is clear: **longer contract commitment strongly suppresses churn**. Customers locked into annual or biennial contracts have much higher switching costs, both financial (early termination fees) and psychological.

## Internet Service Type Analysis

Internet service type also shows significant churn variation:

| Internet Service | Churn Rate (approx.) |
|-----------------|---------------------|
| Fiber optic | Highest |
| DSL | Moderate |
| No service | Lowest |

<Info>
Fiber optic customers churn at a disproportionately high rate. This counterintuitive finding — where the premium service has the worst retention — suggests that fiber optic customers may have higher expectations, experience greater price sensitivity, or face more competitive alternatives in their market.
</Info>

Fiber optic represents 44% of the customer base, making it a critical segment to address with targeted retention programs.

## Payment Method Analysis

Payment method serves as a proxy for customer engagement and payment automation:

| Payment Method | Profile |
|---------------|--------|
| Electronic check | Highest churn — manual, non-committed payment behavior |
| Mailed check | Elevated churn — manual payment |
| Bank transfer (automatic) | Lower churn — automated, committed |
| Credit card (automatic) | Lower churn — automated, committed |

<Tip>
Customers using automatic payment methods (bank transfer or credit card) show significantly lower churn rates. Encouraging customers to switch to automatic billing can serve as a low-cost retention mechanism.
</Tip>

## Gender, Partner, and Dependents

Demographic variables show weaker associations with churn:

| Variable | Finding |
|----------|--------|
| Gender (Male/Female) | Minimal difference in churn rates — gender is not a meaningful predictor |
| Partner (Yes/No) | Customers with partners churn slightly less |
| Dependents (Yes/No) | Customers with dependents churn less — household accounts are stickier |
| Senior Citizen (65+) | Senior customers show elevated churn rates |

<Note>
Gender was binarized in the dataset (Female = 1, Male = 0) for downstream modeling purposes, but the countplot analysis confirms it has near-zero predictive power for churn.
</Note>
