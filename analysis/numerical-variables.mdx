## Overview

Numerical variable analysis uses boxplots and KDE (kernel density estimation) plots to compare the distribution of continuous features between customers who churned and those who stayed. Four variables are examined: contract tenure (months), monthly charges, total charges, and daily charges.

## Visualization Code

The notebook generates a 2×2 grid of boxplots for all numerical variables, plus a separate KDE density plot for tenure:

```python
import matplotlib.pyplot as plt
import seaborn as sns

# 1. Definición de las variables numéricas de interés
vars_numericas = ['Meses_Contrato', 'Cargo_Mensual', 'Cargo_Total', 'Cargo_Diario']

# 2. Configuración de la figura (Matriz de 2x2 para Boxplots)
fig, axes = plt.subplots(nrows=2, ncols=2, figsize=(16, 12))
axes = axes.flatten()

for i, var in enumerate(vars_numericas):
    # Boxplot para ver medianas y la dispersión del gasto/tiempo
    sns.boxplot(data=df_telecom_es, x='Abandono', y=var,
                palette='Set2', ax=axes[i])

    axes[i].set_title(f'Distribución de {var} vs Abandono', fontsize=14, fontweight='bold')
    axes[i].set_xlabel('¿Hubo Abandono? (0=No, 1=Sí)')
    axes[i].set_ylabel(var)

plt.tight_layout()
plt.show()

# 3. Análisis de Densidad para 'Meses_Contrato' (Variable crítica)
plt.figure(figsize=(10, 5))
sns.kdeplot(data=df_telecom_es, x='Meses_Contrato', hue='Abandono',
            fill=True, common_norm=False, palette='crest', alpha=0.5)
plt.title('Densidad de Abandono según Meses de Contrato', fontsize=14, fontweight='bold')
plt.xlabel('Meses de Contrato (tenure)')
plt.ylabel('Densidad de Clientes')
plt.show()
```

## Tenure Analysis (Meses_Contrato)

Tenure is the **strongest predictor** of churn in the dataset.

| Segment | Median Tenure |
|---------|---------------|
| Churned customers | ~10 months |
| Retained customers | ~38 months |

<Warning>
Churn risk is **extremely high during the first 6 months** of service. The KDE density plot shows a sharp churn peak in the early tenure range. After this critical window, churn density drops substantially for customers who stay.
</Warning>

Key observations:
- Customers who churned have a median tenure roughly 4× lower than those who stayed
- The distribution of churned customers is heavily right-skewed, with the bulk leaving within the first year
- Long-tenured customers (5+ years) almost never churn, confirming strong loyalty effects over time

**Implication:** The highest-leverage retention investments should target **new customers in their first 3–6 months** of service.

## Monthly Charges Analysis (Cargo_Mensual)

Monthly charges show a **positive correlation** with churn — customers paying more per month are more likely to leave.

| Segment | Median Monthly Charge |
|---------|----------------------|
| Churned customers | ~$79/month |
| Retained customers | ~$65/month |

| Statistic | All Customers |
|-----------|---------------|
| Mean | $64.76 |
| Median | $70.35 |
| Min | $18.25 |
| Max | $118.75 |
| Std Dev | $30.09 |

<Info>
The higher median monthly charge for churned customers indicates **price sensitivity**. When customers pay a premium but don't perceive equivalent value — especially without add-on services like tech support or online security — they are more likely to seek cheaper alternatives.
</Info>

## Total Charges Analysis (Cargo_Total)

Total charges show a **negative correlation** with churn — customers with higher total spending (i.e., long-tenured customers) are less likely to leave.

| Segment | Median Total Charges |
|---------|---------------------|
| Churned customers | ~$700 |
| Retained customers | ~$1,800 |

| Statistic | All Customers |
|-----------|---------------|
| Mean | $2,279.73 |
| Median | $1,394.55 |
| Min | $0.00 |
| Max | $8,684.80 |
| Std Dev | $2,266.79 |

<Tip>
The apparent paradox — higher monthly charges increase churn while higher total charges decrease it — resolves cleanly: **total charges are a proxy for tenure**. Customers who have been with the company for years naturally accumulate high total charges. It is their long relationship (loyalty), not the total amount spent, that reduces churn risk.
</Tip>

## Daily Charges Feature (Cargo_Diario)

The `Cargo_Diario` (daily charge) feature was engineered during the transformation phase:

## Summary: Numerical Variable Churn Signals

| Variable | Direction | Interpretation |
|----------|-----------|----------------|
| Tenure (`Meses_Contrato`) | Negative correlation with churn (−0.35) | Longer customers churn less — the #1 retention indicator |
| Monthly Charges (`Cargo_Mensual`) | Positive correlation with churn (+0.19) | Higher bills increase churn risk |
| Total Charges (`Cargo_Total`) | Negative correlation with churn (−0.20) | Proxy for tenure; long-term customers spend more overall |
| Daily Charges (`Cargo_Diario`) | Positive correlation with churn (+0.19) | Mirrors monthly charge behavior |

<Note>
These correlation values are from Pearson correlation analysis conducted in the final correlation section of the notebook. Tenure's −0.35 correlation is the largest magnitude of any single variable, making it the dominant numerical predictor of customer retention.
</Note>
