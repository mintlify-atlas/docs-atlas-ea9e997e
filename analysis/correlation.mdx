## Overview

The correlation analysis examines linear relationships between numerical variables — including the binary-encoded churn target — to quantify which features most strongly predict customer attrition. This section corresponds to the "Extra" analysis section in the notebook's final report.

## Encoding Binary Variables for Correlation

Before computing the correlation matrix, Yes/No categorical variables were encoded as binary integers (1/0) during the standardization step:

```python
import pandas as pd

# Transformación de valores textuales a binarios (0 y 1)
# Esto facilita el procesamiento matemático para modelos de Machine Learning
mapeo_binario = {'Yes': 1, 'No': 0, 'Si': 1, 'Female': 1, 'Male': 0}

# Columnas con valores "Yes/No" o similares
columnas_a_binarizar = [
    'Abandono', 'Tiene_Pareja', 'Tiene_Dependientes',
    'Servicio_Telefonico', 'Factura_Digital', 'Genero'
]

for col in columnas_a_binarizar:
    if col in df_telecom_es.columns:
        df_telecom_es[col] = df_telecom_es[col].map(mapeo_binario).fillna(df_telecom_es[col])

print("Estandarización y transformación completadas.")
print("\n--- Vista previa de datos transformados (Binarios) ---")
display(df_telecom_es[['ID_Cliente', 'Abandono', 'Genero', 'Cargo_Mensual']].head())
```

**Output:**

<Note>
Mapping: `Female = 1`, `Male = 0`, `Yes = 1`, `No = 0`. This encoding allows Pearson correlation to measure linear associations between these binary variables and the churn target.
</Note>

## Correlation Heatmap

The notebook generates a heatmap of the Pearson correlation matrix for all numerical and binarized features. The standard approach using seaborn:

```python
import matplotlib.pyplot as plt
import seaborn as sns

# Seleccionar variables numéricas y binarias para correlación
numerical_cols = [
    'Abandono', 'Meses_Contrato', 'Cargo_Mensual',
    'Cargo_Total', 'Cargo_Diario', 'Adulto_Mayor',
    'Tiene_Pareja', 'Tiene_Dependientes'
]

corr_matrix = df_telecom_es[numerical_cols].corr()

plt.figure(figsize=(12, 10))
sns.heatmap(
    corr_matrix,
    annot=True,
    fmt='.2f',
    cmap='coolwarm',
    center=0,
    square=True,
    linewidths=0.5
)
plt.title('Matriz de Correlación — Factores de Abandono',
          fontsize=14, fontweight='bold')
plt.tight_layout()
plt.show()
```

## Tenure: The Dominant Retention Factor

The correlation between tenure and churn (−0.35) is the largest-magnitude correlation in the matrix and confirms what the categorical and distribution analyses suggest:

> **The longer a customer has been with TelecomX, the lower their probability of churning.**

This relationship reflects several compounding dynamics:
- Long-tenured customers have already survived the high-risk early period
- They have accumulated services, data, and integrations that raise switching costs
- They have established a payment routine and relationship with the provider

## Monthly vs. Total Charges: The Apparent Paradox

At first glance, it seems contradictory that monthly charges correlate positively with churn (+0.19) while total charges correlate negatively (−0.20). The resolution:

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Monthly Charges (+0.19)" icon="credit-card">
    Higher monthly bills create **price pressure**. Customers paying more per month are more likely to compare competitors and switch if they don't perceive proportional value.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Total Charges (−0.20)" icon="chart-line">
    Total charges are a **tenure proxy**. A customer who has spent $8,000 in total has been with the company for years — their low churn risk comes from loyalty, not from the spending itself.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## The "Ecosystem Effect": Number of Services

The notebook also examines a derived variable `Num_Servicios` that sums all service subscriptions (Internet, Security, Support, Streaming, etc.):

> **Insight:** Customers with only 1 or 2 services have very high churn rates. Starting from 3–4 services contracted, the churn rate stabilizes or decreases.

This "ecosystem effect" suggests that **integration depth** is a retention driver. The more services a customer uses, the higher their perceived switching cost and the lower their likelihood of leaving.

## Strategic Recommendations from Correlation Analysis

<Info>
The correlation analysis directly informs the priority retention strategy: detect high-risk customers early and anchor them into the ecosystem before they reach the churn decision point.
</Info>

Key data-driven recommendations from the notebook:

1. **Target new customers in the first 3 months** — tenure is the dominant churn predictor, and the risk is highest at the start
2. **Offer free or discounted Tech Support** to high-charge customers in their first contract period — reducing perceived price pressure while increasing service integration
3. **Promote automatic payment methods** — customers with automated billing show lower churn rates (and electronic check users show the highest)
4. **Encourage contract upgrades** — moving month-to-month customers to annual contracts dramatically reduces churn probability (from 42.7% to 11.3%)
5. **Set alerts for customers** with monthly charges above the 80th percentile of their segment — these customers are the most price-sensitive and most at risk
